## The situation

The work order was narrow: investigate a handful of Microsoft 365 oddities. Outlook behaving strangely, files overwriting each other, things nobody could explain. Within the first week it was clear the M365 issues were symptoms. Aging on-premise infrastructure, no foundational IT governance, admin accounts used for everyday email, no record of who had privileged access or when they used it, and a security surface nobody had ever measured.

The general manager had executive sponsorship and was ready to act. What the organization needed was a defensible, framework-anchored picture of actual risk, not another vendor pitch dressed up as a report.

## How the assessment ran

**Scope expanded when the narrow scope stopped making sense.** The engagement became a full assessment covering governance, service desk, infrastructure, data, and security. Roughly 16 servers, 80 endpoints, and 15 business applications in scope.

**Evidence, not questionnaires.** Vulnerability scans across three internal network segments. Forensic imaging and offline analysis of laptops and phones. Firewall configuration export and log review. On-site walkthroughs of the server room, wiring closets, and guest Wi-Fi. Interviews with the GM, the IT manager, and the hands-on technician. Two devices came back forensically confirmed as compromised.

**Fixes did not wait for the report.** The firewall's management interface was found exposed to the public internet; IT disabled external access the same day. Firmware carrying actively exploited vulnerabilities was scheduled for upgrade during the engagement. A loaner log server extended firewall log retention from 24 hours to a window long enough to actually investigate an incident.

**The deliverable is a working document.** Every finding mapped into a traceable matrix: the finding, the action, the specific CIS Controls it satisfies, and the evidence that proves the fix took. A gloss for the non-IT reader: the CIS Controls are a prioritized catalog of security safeguards, and the baseline tier (IG1) is the set every organization needs first, things like multi-factor authentication, limiting admin rights, patching, central logs, and tested backups. The 38-item roadmap was framed as a 90-day trajectory with partner support, or 120 to 180 days with internal staff.

## What held up

- Executive leadership got a defensible, current-state picture across governance, infrastructure, data, and security.
- Critical exposures were closed during the engagement, not after it.
- The matrix became a living ledger: owners, dates, status, and monthly metrics like MFA coverage, patch percentage, and time-to-close on known vulnerabilities.
